The best hand is selected using ALL 3 of the player's own cards plus any 2 cards showing on the table, including cards revealed by players who may have folded in betting rounds 2 and 3. The highest 5 card hand wins.

A seven card stud game with the pot split between the highest hand and the highest spade in the hole (face up spades do not count for the highest spade).

Deal: 2 cards face down, 4 cards face up and last card face down. Betting rounds after each face up card deal and the final face down deal. Each betting round is begun by the player with the highest hand showing.

After final betting round, active players declare wither they are going for highest (face down) spade, highest hand or both. A player declaring both wins only if holding the highest spade and the highest hand.

The highest 5 card hand and highest face down spade split the pot.

Deal: 2 cards face down and 1 card face up to each player. A betting round starting with the highest face up hand. The deck is then placed in the middle of the table and 2 cards are turned up and placed on each side of the deck. Each player in turn selects a card, either one of the face up cards or a card from the top of the deck, which they would turn up except if it is the 7th card. As turned up cards are removed, the dealer replaces them with a card from the top of the deck. After all players have selected a card, there is a betting round. Play continues in this manner until all players have 6 cards. For the 7th card, players selected a turned up card, keep it face up, players that select from the top of the deck keep the card face down. After all players have 7 cards, there will either be 1 or 2 turned up cards remaining, depending whether the last card taken is an up card or down card. These central cards are out of play, but known to the players during the final betting round.

The highest 5 card hand wins.
